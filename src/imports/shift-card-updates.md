1. Remove the colored vertical border from shift cards.

Shift cards currently have a colored left border in addition to a colored progress bar.

Remove the left colored border.

The progress bar already communicates shift workload and status, so the border creates redundant visual signals and increases visual noise.

Shift cards should remain neutral with only the progress bar indicating load.

---

2. Add a shift marketplace indicator.

If a shift was taken from the shift marketplace (біржа змін), display a small marketplace indicator on the shift card.

Placement:
Top-right corner of the shift card.

Style:
Small icon representing shift exchange / marketplace.

The indicator should be subtle and not look like a warning or alert.

Purpose:
This communicates that the shift was filled via the marketplace rather than assigned directly.

---

3. Differentiate employee origin badges.

Employees working in another department or store should have origin badges next to their role information.

Differentiate the two cases visually:

Another department in the same store:
Blue badge with the department name.

Example:
[Каса]

Another store:
Purple badge with the store name.

Example:
[Сільпо №124]

This distinction helps managers quickly identify whether the employee is from the same store or from another location.

---

4. Signal hierarchy.

Ensure each visual signal communicates a different concept:

Shift card indicator:
Shift source (marketplace).

Employee badge:
Employee origin (department or store).

Progress bar:
Workload within the shift.

Avoid using multiple visual signals for the same information.
